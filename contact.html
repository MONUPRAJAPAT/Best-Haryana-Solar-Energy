Common Questions

Things customers ask us all the time.

Straight answers to the things most homeowners and businesses want to know before going solar.

What's the approximate cost per kW?

For a standard on-grid rooftop in Haryana, typical cost is around ₹60,000 to ₹70,000 per kW before subsidy. After PM Surya Ghar, the effective cost drops significantly — for example, a 3 kW system at ₹1,95,000 becomes roughly ₹1,17,000 net after the ₹78,000 subsidy. Exact pricing depends on panel and inverter brands and structure height.

How does the PM Surya Ghar subsidy actually work?

You apply on the official PM Surya Ghar portal with your DISCOM consumer number. We register your application, get technical feasibility approval, install the system, and submit commissioning documents. Once the DISCOM installs the net meter and approves, the subsidy is credited directly to your bank account by the central government. We handle all of this for you.

What warranty comes with the panels and inverter?

Panels carry a 25-year performance warranty and 10 to 12-year product warranty from Tata Power Solar, Waaree, Adani, Vikram Solar etc. String and hybrid inverters carry 5 to 10-year warranty depending on brand (Luminous, Microtek, Havells). Mounting structure is hot-dip galvanized for 20+ years of life.

How much maintenance does it really need?

Honestly, very little. The main thing is cleaning the panels with water every 15 to 30 days, especially in dusty areas. Apart from that, an annual visual check of cables and connectors is enough. We include 5 years of routine maintenance support with every system we install.

How long does installation take?

The physical installation of a typical residential system (1 to 10 kW) is completed in just 1 to 2 days. The rest of the process — subsidy approval, DISCOM inspection and net meter commissioning — is usually wrapped up within 10 to 15 days.

What is net metering and how does it help me?

Net metering installs a bidirectional meter that measures both the power you draw from the grid and the surplus solar power you send back. At the end of each billing cycle, you only pay for the net consumption. Generate more than you use? Those units get credited and offset future bills.

What happens during a power cut?

A standard on-grid system shuts off during a power cut for safety (DISCOM regulation). If you need backup during cuts, we recommend a hybrid system with battery storage — that keeps fans, lights, TV, and fridge running. We can size battery capacity to your backup needs.

What's the payback period and real ROI?

For most residential customers in Haryana, payback is 3 to 5 years after the PM Surya Ghar subsidy. The system continues generating free electricity for another 20+ years — which is why total 25-year savings often cross ₹5 lakh to ₹10 lakh depending on system size.
